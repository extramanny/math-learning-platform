Combinatorics: Permutations

Step 1: Learn the Concept

A permutation is an arrangement of objects in a specific order.

Formula: \(P(n,r) = \frac{n!}{(n-r)!}\)

Where n is the total number of items and r is the number of items being arranged.

Example: How many ways can we arrange 3 letters from ABC?

Solution: \(P(3,3) = \frac{3!}{(3-3)!} = \frac{3!}{0!} = \frac{6}{1} = 6\)

The arrangements are: ABC, ACB, BAC, BCA, CAB, CBA
